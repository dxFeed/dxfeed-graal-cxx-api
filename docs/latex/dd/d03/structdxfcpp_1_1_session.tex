\doxysubsubsection{Detailed Description}
{\bfseries{\doxylink{structdxfcpp_1_1_session}{Session}}} represents a continuous period of time during which apply same rules regarding trading activity. 

For example, {\ttfamily regular trading session} is a period of time consisting of one day of business activities in a financial market, from the opening bell to the closing bell, when regular trading occurs. 

Sessions can be either {\bfseries{trading}} or {\bfseries{non-\/trading}}, with different sets of rules and reasons to exist. Sessions do not overlap with each other -\/ rather they form consecutive chain of adjacent periods of time that cover entire time scale. The point on a border line is considered to belong to following session that starts there. Each session completely fits inside a certain day. \doxylink{structdxfcpp_1_1_day}{Day} may contain sessions with zero duration -\/ e.\+g. indices that post value once a day. Such sessions can be of any appropriate type, trading or non-\/trading.
